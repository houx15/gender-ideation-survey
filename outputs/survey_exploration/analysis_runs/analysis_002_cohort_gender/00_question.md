# analysis_002 — Gender ideation by birth cohort and gender (CFPS)

**Research question:** Is the secular decline in traditional gender ideology a
*cohort* pattern, and does the gender gap differ across cohorts? (Context for
SPEC 5.1 measurement and 5.2 individual practice.)

**Analysis type:** Descriptive + exploratory modelling (individual practice).

**Data / years / sample:** CFPS 2014 and 2020 adult files; respondents with ≥1
valid ideation item, valid birth year (1930–2004), and known gender.

**Core variables:**
- Outcome: gender-ideation index ([0,1], 1 = most traditional).
- Explanatory: birth cohort (decades, centered at 1970) and gender (female=1).
- Interaction: cohort × female.

**Why birth cohort:** cohort is fixed at birth and therefore precedes the attitude
measurement — unlike education or marriage outcomes, it carries no reverse-causation
/ time-ordering problem (SPEC 12.7).
