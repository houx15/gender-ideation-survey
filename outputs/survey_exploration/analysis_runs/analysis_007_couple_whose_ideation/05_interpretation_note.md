# analysis_007 — Interpretation note

## The answer: it depends what you mean by "matters" — and the picture is asymmetric
From `whose_ideation_comparison.csv` / `04_result_table.csv` (10,675 couples).

1. **For the wife's own housework burden, HER ideology dominates.**
   `wife_housework_hrs`: wife-ideation **+0.97 hrs (t=8.1)** vs husband-ideation +0.23 (t=2.0)
   — the wife's ideology has **~4× the effect** of her husband's. A traditional woman
   takes on much more housework herself.
2. **The husband's housework barely responds to anyone's ideology.**
   `husband_housework_hrs`: wife-ideation +0.09 (ns), husband-ideation −0.11 (ns).
   Men's contribution is "sticky" regardless of either spouse's beliefs.
3. **For the relative SHARE, both matter — and the husband's is slightly larger.**
   `wife_share`: wife-ideation +0.079 (t=4.9), husband-ideation **+0.102 (t=6.8)**.
   A traditional husband raises his wife's share about as much as (a touch more than) a
   traditional wife does.

## So does women's ideology "matter more" in the family?
**For her own labour, decisively yes** — a woman's ideology is highly self-consequential
(consistent with analysis_004/005, where her ideology predicts her housework and
employment while men's barely respond). **But the household division does not become more
equal just because she is egalitarian**: because men's housework hardly moves, shifting
the *share* depends at least as much on the husband's ideology. Equality seems to require
an egalitarian *husband*, not only an egalitarian wife.

Mean division: wife 2.9 hrs vs husband 1.5 hrs/day; wife's share ≈ 69%. Spouse ideation
correlation 0.22 (assortative).

## Threats / caveats
- Associational; reverse causation; mechanical asymmetry (wives do more → more variance).
- No formal wife=husband coefficient test yet (needs joint covariance).
- Married, co-resident, unweighted; CFPS index reliability modest.
