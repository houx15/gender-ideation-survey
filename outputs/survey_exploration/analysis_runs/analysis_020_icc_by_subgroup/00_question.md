# analysis_020 — Sibling ICC of ideology by urban/rural & cohort (CFPS, SPEC 5.7)

**Research question:** Is gender ideology more family-clustered (siblings more alike) in
rural vs urban families, and in older vs younger sibships? Does the share of sibling
resemblance explained by parents' measured ideology vary across these contexts?

**Analysis type:** Variance-components decomposition (extends analysis_018).

**Data / sample:** CFPS 2014 & 2020; real multi-child families (≥2 sampled siblings sharing
both in-sample parents). Subgroups: rural vs urban (family majority urban), older vs younger
sibship (split at the median family-mean child age, a cohort proxy).

**Quantities:** sibling ICC of child ideation per subgroup, raw and after partialling out
`parent_mean` ideology; the difference = share of resemblance attributable to measured
parent ideology.
